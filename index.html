01 — About

I am a public health researcher and epidemiologist working at the intersection of mortality surveillance, cause-of-death analysis, and health-systems strengthening.

My doctoral research at the University of Gothenburg marked the first national application of the verbal autopsy method in Saudi Arabia—generating actionable evidence on deaths related to type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular disease, two of the Kingdom's leading health priorities. Between 2020 and 2025, I conducted large-scale fieldwork across hospitals and communities, analysing over 12,000 mortality records and carrying out more than 300 verbal autopsy interviews.

That work exposed inequities in healthcare access and data quality, integrating community perspectives with epidemiological data to inform national policy and strengthen mortality-statistics systems. I am drawn to questions where measurement and equity meet: who is counted, who is missed, and how better data can change the decisions that shape population health.

Today, as Assistant Professor of Public Health at Umm Al-Qura University and Head of Health Administration and Medical Informatics, I combine research, teaching, and institutional leadership—bridging epidemiological rigour with the practical realities of health systems in Saudi Arabia and beyond.

Research projects

Doctoral Programme · 2020–2025

National Verbal Autopsy Application in Saudi Arabia

The first national application of the verbal autopsy method in the Kingdom, built on a sub-national type 2 diabetes register to recover cause-of-death evidence for deaths occurring outside the formal hospital system.

  • Conduct 300+ verbal autopsy interviews across hospital and community settings
  • Integrate community perceptions of cause of death with epidemiological data
  • Generate actionable national evidence on diabetes- and CVD-related mortality
